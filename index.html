Interesting Facts About Camels

Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • There are more than 14 million camels in the world – most of them live in the Middle East, Asia and Australia.
  • Camels aren’t as slow as they look – they can run up to speeds of 40mph. However, they cannot maintain this for very long, but can comfortably move at up to 25mph.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

Chameleon

  • Chameleon change color for camouflage but this is not always the main reason. Some show darker colors when angry, or when trying to scare others.
  • The chameleons' eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs, so they can see two different objects at the same time. This gives them a full 360-degree field of vision.
  • The chameleons tongue can reach its prey in just 0.07 split seconds, with the projectile acceleration reaching over 41 g's of force.
  • Almost half of the world's chameleon species are native to Madagascar.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
  • By delivering critical updates, pigeons have saved thousands of human lives during WW1 and WW2.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears

Bear

  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss.
  • The world's largest stitched teddy bear is proudly American! The 55-foot-4 creation was made in 2008 and is named C.T.Dreams, which stands for Connect the Dreams.

Interesting Facts About Pandas

Panda

  • Pandas are omnivores. But whilst pandas will occasionally eat small animals and fish, bamboo counts for 99 percent of their diet.
  • An adult Panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago. Fossils of pandas have been dated between 1 and 2 million years old.
  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm – that’s about the size of a pencil! They are also born blind and only open their eyes six to eight weeks after birth.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • Cicadas can live a relatively long time: 4 to 17 years, depending on whether they are annual or periodical cicadas. The periodical cicadas live the longest.
  • Though a group of these insects are called a cloud or plague, cicadas usually don't cause the harm that locusts cause to crops and local vegetation.
  • Some cicada calls can be heard up to 1 mile (1.5 kilometer) away.
